How to use the app?

The unit price is the cost per liter or per gram of the item you want to buy.

For example, let's say you're hungry for pancakes, and need to buy some flour. In the shop, you have two choices: a smaller package - $1 for 1 kg, and a larger one - $1.35 for 1500 g. Which one is a better buy?.

Thanks to our price per unit calculator, you can just type the details of the first product onto the First Volume and Cost (A), and type the details of the second product on Second Volume and Cost (B). Then press compute, and now you know that the unit price for the smaller package is $1/kg, while for the larger package it is $0.90/kg.

When you compare two unit prices, you can easily calculate how much you save compared to the item sold at the higher price - in our example: $0.10.
